Latinos in Architecture forum on Wednesday November 2, 2011 6-9:00PM at UT’s Goldsmith Hall (School of Architecture Building)

Monday, October 31, 2011

The design charrette is being held in association with a symposium with with the “Universidad de Albert Einstein” (UAE) from Mexico City and facilitated through the UT Latin American Studies and Planning Departments. The purpose of the symposium is to help UAE’s faculty and administrators develop a curriculum centered on sustainability and peace education, and at the same time integrate this into an overall planning concept for the University that ties together green strategies for the campus and engages the local community. Design Charette is scheduled for Wednesday, November 2nd, 6-9:00PM at UT’s Goldsmith Hall (School of Architecture Building) Directors and faculty members from UAE will participate alongside faculty members from UT Austin.

The following guests/groups have been invited to assist in the discussion:
· Dick Peterson - Rainwater · John Umphress - Micro hydro generation
· Patrica Michael & Dick Pierce- Permaculture · Travis Glenn, Laine Hardy, Ropert Gaspard- Climate Profile
· AIA Austin’s Committee on the Environment (Heidi Kasper AIA, Co-chair)
· AIA Austin’s Latinos in Architecture Committee (Jaime Palomo AIA, Chair)

People with knowledge and experience in any of the following areas are encouraged to attend and participate:
* Permaculture * Transportation design * Daylighting * Campus design

EWB-AUS at Foro Agua Santa

Friday, September 23, 2011

Long-term volunteers James Seppi and Taylor Cook represented the EWB-AUS Peru Program at the Foro Agua Santa 2011 held in Huaraz, Anchash, Peru on September 22 and 23. The forum was a venue for water users at all levels of the Santa River Basin, from the peaks to the coast, to share their concerns and ideas about how to implement integrated management of this important resource.

Taylor and James met with forum attendees and handed out information to promote the Peru Program.

End of Peru Program Assessment 1 Trip

Tuesday, August 16, 2011

Today marks the end of the Peru Program's first Assessment Trip.  The assessment team visited two communities of the Tres Cuencas Commonwealth: Canrey Chico and Huasta.  In addition to signing the Program MOU, the team met with local researchers, scientists, professors, mayors, and community members to learn about water problems and potential first projects.   They look forward to sharing their findings, data, and pictures with the rest of the Peru Program Team and the EWB-AUS Board!
